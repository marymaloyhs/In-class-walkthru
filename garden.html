How to Create a Garden in a College Dorm

Introduction

Creating a small garden in your college dorm is a great way to bring life, color, and calm into your living space. Even with limited room and strict housing rules, you can grow herbs, small vegetables, or decorative plants using simple containers and basic gardening methods. Dorm gardening can also help improve air quality and reduce stress.

What You’ll Need

  • Small pots or containers with drainage holes
  • Potting soil (never use outdoor dirt)
  • Easy-to-grow plants such as basil, mint, or succulents
  • A small watering can or cup
  • A sunny windowsill or small grow light

Step-by-Step Guide

Start by choosing plants that grow well indoors and do not require a lot of space. Herbs and leafy greens are excellent options for beginners. Fill your containers with potting soil, leaving space at the top so water does not overflow.

Gently plant your seeds or seedlings according to the instructions on the label. Place your plants near a window that receives several hours of sunlight each day. Water the soil lightly after planting to help roots settle.

Basic Plant Care

  • Water plants when the top inch of soil feels dry
  • Rotate pots every few days so plants grow evenly
  • Remove dead leaves to prevent mold or pests
  • Avoid overwatering, which can cause root rot

Consistent care is more important than perfection. Simple routines such as checking moisture levels and keeping leaves dust-free can greatly improve plant health in a dorm environment.
